    6. Chapter 13: Simulation & Modeling

    Define these terms:
    1. scientific method
      2. the process of objectively establishing facts through testing and experimentation.
    2. computational model
      3. Computational modeling is the use of computers to simulate and study complex systems using mathematics, physics and computer science.
    3. discrete event simulation
      4. a method used to model real world systems that can be decomposed into a set of logically separate processes that autonomously progress through time.
    4. List and describe seven reasons to construct a simulation model.
      5. Prediction: Simulation models can be used to predict the future behavior of a system.
      Optimization: Simulation models can be used to optimize the performance of a system by identifying the optimal settings for various parameters.
      Training and education: Simulation models can be used to provide training and education to personnel who will operate or work with a system.
      Risk management: Simulation models can be used to assess and manage risks in complex systems or processes.
      Decision making: Simulation models can be used to support decision making by providing insights and information about the behavior of a system.
      Cost savings: Simulation models can be used to identify cost-saving opportunities by optimizing the use of resources or reducing waste.
      Experimentation: Simulation models can be used to conduct experiments that would be difficult or impossible to conduct in the real world.
