Briefly describe or define:

  1. information security
    2. The term 'information security' means protecting information and information systems from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction in order to provide integrity, confidentiality, and availability.
  2. authentication
    3. you supply or confirm credentials that are unique to you to prove that you are exactly who you declare to be.
  3. authorization
    4. The process of verifying that a requested action or service is approved for a specific entity
  4. dual authentication
    5. an extra layer of security used when logging into websites or apps.
  5. social engineering
    6. all techniques aimed at talking a target into revealing specific information or performing a specific action for illegitimate reasons.
  6. virus
    7. a type of malicious software, or malware, that spreads between computers and causes damage to data and software.
  7. worm
    8. a type of malware whose primary function is to self-replicate and infect other computers while remaining active on infected systems
  8. Trojan horse
    9. a type of malware that downloads onto a computer disguised as a legitimate program
  9. ransomware
    10. a form of malware designed to encrypt files on a device, rendering any files and the systems that rely on them unusable.
  10. phishing
    11. an attempt to trick victims into. sharing sensitive information such as passwords, usernames, and credit card details for malicious reasons.
  11. White Hat
    12. a computer hacker who is hired by an organization to undertake nonmalicious hacking work in order to discover computer-security flaws.
  12. Black Hat
    13. criminals who break into computer networks with malicious intent.
  13. encryption/decryption
    14. Encrypted secrets allow you to store sensitive information in your organization, repository, or repository environments. What is Decryption. Definition: The conversion of encrypted data into its original form
  14. cryptography
    15. the practice and study of techniques for secure communication in the presence of adversarial behavior.
  15. symmetric encryption algorithm
    16. uses one key to encrypt and decrypt
  16. Caesar cipher
    17. a shift cipher and encrypts the data by replacing the original letters with “x” number of characters ahead in the alphabet.
  17. block cipher
    18. a method of encrypting data in blocks to produce ciphertext using a cryptographic key and algorithm.
  18. Data Encryption Standard (DES)
    19. a block cipher, meaning a cryptographic key and algorithm are applied to a block of data simultaneously rather than one bit at a time.
  19. public key system
    20. a method of encrypting or signing data with two different keys and making one of the keys, the public key, available for anyone to use.
  20. embedded computers
    21. a combination of computer hardware and software designed for a specific function.
